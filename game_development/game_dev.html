Shadow v2

I recently found unfinished or partially finished game projects in my dropbox. Many of these are unfinished projects which served to demonstrate my ability to code. However, one of these I considered finished.

Shadow v2 was the final game I developed alone for Advanced Game Design during my sophomore year of high school. Developed in Game Maker 8, a free video game engine for beginners, most of the objects could be created with drag and drop code via ~70 premade actions, but I opted to code with Game Maker Language (GML) scripts if more than 3 actions were needed. Here is a link to my dropbox to download and play the executable.

In this game the objective is to navigate each level without entering any light and delluminate all torches to expose stairs to ascend to the next level. The player can shoot shadows at the torches to interact with them. There are three variants of torches: yellow, red, and blue.

  • Yellow torches are surrounded by lights which disappear when unlit
  • Red torches come in pairs and relight shortly after becoming unlit unless both are unlit together
  • Blue torches when unlit empower shadows to destroy lights

Through the initial project I demonstrated my comfort in GML with sprites, animation, collisions, timed events, and basic coding. The Game Maker project from 2014 (*.gmk file) could not be played without expired software and only included 3 playable levels. So, I decided to add 5 levels to better showcase the game mechanics before compiling the project into an executable file.


Fishing Game

Stepping aside from the below Dungeon Delve project, I am working on a smaller scope project to submit to Potions & Pixels One Button Contest Game Jam. The theme of this Game Jam is to complete a game with only one assigned input for the game, and it is limited to participants local to the area. I am working on the core gameplay loop while my brother works on modelling, animations and visuals. The guidelines for the Game Jam are as follows

  • One dedicated button used for all input (excluding non-gameplay menus)
  • Participation limited to residents within greater Charlotte metropolitan area
  • Submission must be uploaded to itch.io
My submission will be a fishing game with all inputs bound to spacebar. The player will cast a line in a body of water targeting fish, then completing a fishing minigame to catch the fish until all fish are removed from the body of water.

Dungeon Delve

I am currently learning how to use Unreal Engine through developing a game. I plan to create a cooperative PvE extraction rpg where you descend a dungeon to higher difficulties before resurfacing. For my first milestone, I am planning to have 5 distinct enemy archetypes with basic AI, combat, 1 boss fight, 5 different themed levels, and an extraction system. Through creating this game, I hope to learn the basics of the 3D game engine to understand where my strengths, limits and weaknesses lie. So far, I have created

  • Rigged basic 3d objects as enemies and weapons
  • Custom animations for enemies and weapons
  • Basic enemy AI using player data
  • Four levels connected by stairs objects
  • Health UI and Damage logic for player and enemies
I consider the next steps towards completion to be my weak points, and I expect research and execution going forward will broaden my horizons of how I view development in Unreal:
  • Sound effects
  • Background music
  • Collisions and physics (combat, movement)
  • GUIs
  • Lighting
  • Enemy AI that interacts with level design
